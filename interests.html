~ANIMES~

Honestly, I am not a fan of watching drama series unless I am very seriously intrigued. Animes, especially in another foreign language, have enabled me to be more open to knowing about another's significant culture as well as the uniqueness that lies underneath. Japanese animes significantly color my life, especially during my teenage years, ever since my sister introduced me to my first anime; Kaichou Wa Maid-sama or in its English title, Maid-sama!.

This anime is a must-watch for those who love suspense and philosophical debate between two people who are actually the two sides of the same coin. It argues the theory of the usage of God when we, human beings, can be a destroyer of our own due to greed and wanting power?

Aptly titled, this anime tells you a story of a brother who has given his very best to make sure his sister becomes herself; not as a demon as she was bitten to, but the young girl who smiles at him every time he comes home. This story is so well-written, filled with interesting plots and stories that will not bore the audience along the way. Most importantly, it teaches that pain as well as suffering can be experienced by anyone, no matter a human being or even a demon, yet it does not give you the right to harm other people for the sake of vengeance or pure greed.

Hanako-Kun is a boy who is also a guardian spirit for the school and handles the rumors that can cause the existence of those spirits around. A girl who is actually not innocent and well-aware of human emotion in depth that even the guardian spirit can't help but to fall in love with her? Not to forget, a monk-in-training who vows to vanquish all spirits, good and bad, yet one day might be exorcising the friend that he has been needing the most in his entire life; forsaking his own love of his life?? Not to mention the plot twists, other side characters that have their own roles and just ghosts-how can someone not watch this masterpiece?

Honestly, one of the animes that are so underrated that it's a criminal act. What do you mean we have a billionaire acting as a detective when in secret he is searching for his parents' murderer while being paired with a weary detective who was actually the best cop in the station? And, did I not mention bomb as well as betrayal with a hint of fluff?

Oh, don't be foolish when the anime tells you about a story of two highschoolers that join a band only. Quite the contrary, this anime tells you a band that will be performing gut-wrenching songs relating to their own personal conflicts as well as trying to understand the depth of grief when it concerns your beloveds. Not only that, these teenagers have experienced heartaches far worse than some adults out there, let me tell you. Fun fact, the characters are inspired by a real-life band that also contributes to being their voice actors, too!
